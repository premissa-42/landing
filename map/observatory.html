Photovoice & Research

Where research meets image. And knowledge becomes form.

Method

Photovoice is a research method where participants take photos in response to research prompts. The photos are not the final outcome — they’re used to guide interviews and discussions. The focus is on what people say about the images, not just the images themselves.

Process

  • Participants receive guiding questions and cameras or use their own
  • Photos are discussed in interviews or group reflections
  • Interviews are anonymised and thematically analysed
  • Findings are translated into public formats like installations or immersive spaces

Why It Matters

This process allows for complex, often underrepresented experiences to surface. It creates knowledge that’s grounded in real life and accessible to others — especially when presented outside traditional academic spaces. At 42, this often takes the form of spatial or visual installations based on anonymised research data.

Examples

In Between (2023)
Research on rurality, queerness, and migration — shown as an immersive installation.
Eu Aqui / Me Here (2022)
Reflections on belonging and place — presented as a sound-photo environment.
Private VR Archive
Anonymous contributions remixed in spatial and digital formats.

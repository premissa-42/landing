Photovoice & Research

A participatory method using photography and storytelling to understand lived experience.

What is Photovoice?

Photovoice is a qualitative research method that combines photography and narrative to explore how people experience their lives and communities. It’s participant-led, accessible, and often used in community-based research and advocacy.

Participants take photos in response to guiding questions or themes, then reflect on their images in group discussions, interviews, or through captions. These photos and stories are then shared through exhibitions, reports, or community presentations.

Why We Use It

  • Participant-led: People represent their own perspectives rather than being interpreted from outside.
  • Visual + narrative: Photos are paired with words to express emotional, social, and cultural insights.
  • Accessible: No need for formal education — participants express themselves through images.
  • Action-oriented: Results can inform public discussion, exhibitions, or policy conversations.

How It Works at 42

  • Intro sessions and ethical training in photography and storytelling
  • Participants create photo series based on shared or personal themes
  • Group discussions to reflect on images and stories
  • Collaborative curation for public exhibition or research output
